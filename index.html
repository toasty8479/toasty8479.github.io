Internet Privacy

This is a guide to teach people steps you can take to stay anonymous online, prevent your information from being stolen, and how you can avoid being tracked between sites and apps.

The 3 apps I will be using as examples frequently throughout this guide are Discord, Steam, and Roblox.

Cross-Platform Identification

First, I'll explain some different ways that you can be tracked between sites, making you identifiable.

Usernames and Discriminators

First, I'll explain what a discriminator actually is. A discriminator, previously used in Discord usernames for example, is a string or set of numbers that makes you uniquely identifiable. So, for example, if you have two friends named 'toasty', you would give each of them a discriminator, like toasty1872 or toasty1242, making it possible to distinguish between them.

Identifiers

Most websites and apps have IDs that make each user unique, similar to Discriminators, an ID is a string of numbers that is able to make an individual identifiable even after they change their username. While it isn't possible to change your ID on any site that I know of other than creating a new account, knowing of it's existance is very important.

A few examples,

  • Roblox's UserId can be found in your profile URL, as shown below: `https://www.roblox.com/users/157350919/profile`
  • Steam has a SteamID that can also be found in the URL of your profile page. `https://steamcommunity.com/profiles /76561199061977736/`
    While it is possible to temporarily hide your SteamID with a CustomID, that makes links appear as `https://steamcommunity.com/id/examplename`, there are websites like [steamid.uk](https://steamid.uk) that take CustomID as input and will output your SteamID.
  • Discord's user ID can be found easily, but it requires the activation of Developer Mode, which is in Settings under the "Developer" section at the very bottom of the settings list. After doing so you can right click anyone's profile and click "Copy User ID" to do so.

IP Address

An IP Address, or IPv4 used most commonly, is a number that uniquely identifies your network. An example of what an IP address could look like is `12.34.56.789`. Bad actors who are looking to obtain your IP are most commonly looking to do a DDoS attack.

DDoS, or Distributed Denial-of-Service, is unfortunately a common practice on the internet that happens when a bad actor overwhelms your network with large chunks of information, which leads to your internet slowing down exponentially, or shutting down entirely.

Cookies

... are pieces of information defined by a website server that are sent to your internet browser to be stored. While this is a completely innocent feature,

For example, if you visit a website and turn it to dark mode and it has configured cookies, it will save that information to your browser so that when you re-visit the website it can fetch that cookie and see if you enabled dark mode on a previous visit to the website.

Tokens

... are strings of information stored inside a cookie that is used to authenticate your identity to a website.

After you log in to a website, it generates a token that allows you to automatically be logged back in upon reopening the page. This feature that most websites offer is one of, if not the most, sought after piece of information by bad actors looking to commit fraud.

Self Investigation

One of the most useful things you can do is actually try to find information about yourself. Try to think of what someone that wants to get your information would use as a start point, and work from there yourself.

Most importantly, when doing research on yourself create a new profile on whatever browser you are using or use an incognito feature to make sure that your results aren't personalized and that you aren't logged in to anything before-hand.

What will make your life exponentially easier when doing so is learning how to manipulate your google searches to be able to find exactly what you are looking for. Platform-Specific Security Measures

For this section I will be providing the ways to be the most private or anonymous on Discord, Steam, and Roblox. For these, I'll go in a pattern: Ensuring current security, tracking prevention, then ensuring future security.

Discord

If you don't want *anyone* to know when you are online, you can appear offline by clicking your profile picture at the bottom left, then clicking or hovering on "Online" and selecting **Invisible**.

Everything listed will be in Discord Settings.This will be ordered from the top of the settings list to the bottom:

  • Under **Account** I reccommend to set-up Multi-Factor Authentication, but it isn't required. Ensure that under **Logged-in Devices** the only devices are owned by you.
  • Under **Content & Social** ensure that **Direct Message spam** is on "Filter for non-friends".
      • Under **Data & Privacy** turn **off**:
    • Use Data to Improve Discord
    • Use my Discord activity to personalize Sponsored Content
    • Use data to personalize my Discord experience
    • Allow my voice to be recorded in Clips
    • Share when I update my profile
  • Under **Authorized Apps** and click **Deauthorize** on everything that isn't *guaranteed to be reputable*. If you believe an application is trustworthy, click the **Permissions** text and look at what permissions the app has for your account.
  • Under **Connections** then turn off **Display on Profile** for anything that is remotely personal. Things like your gaming profiles should be fine but no social media should be shown on your profile.
  • Under **Activity Privacy** turn off **Share my activity** so when you appear online no one is able to see what you are doing.
